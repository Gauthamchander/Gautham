Designing a Seamless Digital Experience for Mia Tanishq Jewelry

A comprehensive UX redesign case study focused on enhancing user engagement, simplifying appointment booking and checkout processes, and improving navigation to create a seamless and user-friendly online experience for the Mia Tanishq jewelry platform.

TEAM

  • Gautham Ravichandran (Lead Product UX/UI Designer)
  • Dhanasekar (Frontend Developer)
  • Nagaraj (Backend Developer)

MY ROLE

Problem Statement

The Mia Tanishq jewelry store faces challenges in creating an engaging and effective online experience. The landing page struggles to communicate the brand's values and uniqueness, leading to high bounce rates and poor user engagement. The appointment fixing and checkout process lacks a seamless and personalized workflow, causing user frustration and abandonment. The store locator lacks intuitive search options and location-based filters, causing user frustration and reducing foot traffic. A streamlined, user-friendly design is needed to improve accessibility and drive in-store visits.

Goals

The number of walk-in customers by streamlining the appointment booking process and Store locator

Simplify the purchasing process, reduce friction, and provide clear steps with multiple payment options, a streamlined checkout, and clear shipping details to decrease cart abandonment and boost conversions

Capture user attention, convey the brand's identity and value, and guide users to explore products or book appointments.

Stakeholder Interview

The stakeholder interviews highlighted the need for a premium online experience that reflects the Mia Tanishq brand, improves appointment bookings, streamlines checkout, and enhances the store locator. The target audience is fashion-conscious women, and pain points include high bounce rates, appointment booking frustrations, and cart abandonment. The platform should feature elegant design, easy navigation, multiple payment options, and a user-friendly store locator. Success will be measured by increased conversions, user engagement, and foot traffic to stores, with future plans for AI-driven recommendations and virtual consultations.

Key takeaways from interviews

Project Focus: Improve Mia Tanishq’s online presence to enhance brand identity, streamline appointment booking, simplify checkout, and provide a user-friendly store locator.

Target Audience & Pain Points: The platform targets fashion-conscious women (ages 25-45) facing issues with high bounce rates, complex appointment booking, and abandoned checkouts.

Design & Features: The design should be elegant, with clear navigation, high-quality visuals, multiple payment options, and an intuitive store locator to drive foot traffic.

Success Metrics & Future Plans: Success will be measured by increased conversions and foot traffic, with plans for AI recommendations and virtual consultations for future enhancements.

User Interview

We conducted interviews with 30 individuals to understand their experiences and preferences regarding jewelry websites, the appointment booking process, store locator functionality, and their purchasing behaviors. The goal was to gather insights on their expectations for a seamless digital experience, identify pain points in the current process, and explore their willingness to engage with a jewelry application.

To ensure demographic diversity, we targeted individuals of different ages, genders, and backgrounds, as jewelry purchasing appeals to a broad audience. We categorized participants into various age groups and genders and conducted individual interviews to gather a wide range of insights, ensuring a comprehensive understanding of user needs and preferences.

Key takeaways from interviews

Shopping Preferences & Challenges: 70% prefer in-store shopping for trust and product visibility, while 30% value online convenience; 50% report frustration with inadequate visuals

Key Features Users Want: 80% prioritize detailed product information, 60% value customer reviews, and 50% seek virtual try-on tools to boost purchase confidence.

Improvements in Booking & Checkout: 65% find appointment booking cumbersome, 55% feel checkout is overly complicated, and 45% want more payment options like installments.

Competitor Analysis

A UX competitor analysis was conducted to identify strengths, weaknesses, and opportunities in the online platforms of leading jewelry brands. The analysis focused on landing pages, product displays, appointment booking workflows, checkout processes, and store locators. Key findings highlighted the importance of robust filtering options, high-quality visuals, AR/VR tools for virtual try-ons, and seamless appointment booking. Insights from the study revealed gaps in competitor platforms, such as inconsistent user flows and missing personalization features, providing actionable recommendations to create a streamlined, user-friendly experience for Mia Tanishq's customers, aligned with the project's goals of increasing engagement, reducing friction, and boosting conversions.

View Competitive Analysis​

Key takeaways Competitor Analysis

Competitors with clean, visually engaging landing pages that effectively communicate brand identity see higher user engagement. Overcrowded or slow-loading designs negatively impact user retention.

Competitors with real-time slot availability and simplified booking workflows provide a smoother experience. Overly lengthy forms or lack of location-based recommendations deter user engagement.

Transparent pricing, multiple payment options, and one-click checkout reduce cart abandonment. Competitors with cumbersome processes or limited payment flexibility struggle to retain customers.

Store locators integrated with geolocation, service filters, and booking options directly from the map provide significant value. Missing features like real-time location updates or user-friendly interfaces frustrate users and reduce foot traffic.

Who are we designing for?

A definition of 3 core user segments which explores their problems, fears, motivations, and potential marketing messages.

From the conversations we had, we spotted three common personas that represent the shared experiences and needs of all our interviewees. This way, we can understand and connect with our users on a more personal level!. These personas guided the design process by uncovering key user pain points and opportunities, ensuring the platform meets the diverse needs of its audience while delivering a refined, user-centered experience.

Key takeaways from user persona

Maya Sharma (32, Marketing Manager):A busy professional seeking a seamless and personalized online jewelry shopping experience. She values convenience, transparent pricing, and features like virtual try-ons to make confident purchases.

Arjun Patel (35, Software Engineer): A tech-savvy buyer focused on gifting jewelry for special occasions. He prioritizes modern, trendy designs, quick navigation, and reliable delivery options for an effortless shopping journey.

Rajesh Gupta (50, Businessman): A cautious, investment-focused buyer who values authenticity, certifications, and expert guidance for high-value purchases. He prefers using online platforms for research but finalizes purchases in physical stores.

User Flow

In my proposal, I focused on streamlining the flow to provide seamless assistance to users during the appointment booking and checkout processes. By simplifying the journey, I ensured that booking appointments is quick and hassle-free, eliminating navigational obstacles. Non-essential sections that didn’t align with the app’s core purpose were removed. The redesigned appointment booking journey removes unnecessary steps like logging in or enabling location settings, which were previously identified as barriers. The proposed design prioritizes delivering essential information at users’ fingertips, making the app intuitive and efficient for users to achieve their goals effortlessly.

Low-fidelity Wireframes

The low-fidelity wireframe for the Mia Tanishq platform is designed to address key user frustrations and align with project goals, focusing on simplicity, clarity, and ease of navigation. It highlights essential features such as a visually compelling landing page, streamlined product exploration with filters and intuitive appointment booking flow without unnecessary barriers, a seamless checkout process with transparent pricing and multiple payment options, and a functional store locator with geolocation and service-based filters. This wireframe serves as the foundation for a user-centric experience, ensuring accessibility, reducing friction, and guiding users toward their desired actions effortlessly.
